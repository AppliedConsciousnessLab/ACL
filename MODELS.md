# ACL Models

## Purpose

This file defines the major conceptual models currently used across the Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL) ecosystem.

Its purpose is to:

- provide a single reference point for ACL diagrams and models
- explain what each model describes
- show how each model fits into the broader ACL framework
- support consistent use across repositories, papers, and public-facing materials

---

# Model Stack Overview

The ACL ecosystem currently uses four primary model layers:

1. Safety and governance models
2. Perception and regulation models
3. Adaptive growth models
4. Social-system failure and recovery models

Together these form the ACL systems architecture.

---

# Figure 1 — The ACL Regulatory Architecture

## Title
**Figure 1 — The ACL Regulatory Architecture**

## Definition
A systems model integrating perception, neurochemical modulation, biological regulation, and adaptive growth.

## Core Logic
The model proposes that human behavior emerges through a layered process:

Reality / Environment  
→ Perception Filter  
→ System State Evaluation  
→ Neurochemical Modulation  
→ Biological Regulation  
→ Human Adaptive Cycle  
→ Behavior / Action  
→ Environmental Feedback

## Why It Matters
This is the umbrella model of ACL. It visually integrates the following:

- The Regulation Principle
- Neurochemical Modulation (NcM)
- The Perception Filter Model
- The Human Adaptive Cycle
- Environmental feedback dynamics

## Canonical Motto
**Safety → Regulation → Agency → Choice**

## Typical Use
Use this model as the primary orientation figure in:

- the main ACL repository
- dissertation and white paper introductions
- Hy-Kin founder reference pages
- orientation materials for new collaborators and AI systems

# The Regulation Principle

## Definition
The foundational ACL principle that health and stability emerge from coherent coordination across interacting regulatory systems.

## Scope
This principle applies across multiple levels:

- biological systems
- psychological systems
- relational systems
- institutional systems
- societal systems

## Core Claim
When regulation is sufficiently coherent, health, flexibility, and adaptation become possible. When regulation degrades, dysfunction and collapse become more likely.

## Typical Use
Use this as the primary interpretive principle across all ACL repositories.

---

# NcM — Neurochemical Modulation

## Definition
A model describing how changes in brain chemistry influence physiological regulation throughout the body.

## Core Components
NcM includes:

- neurotransmitter patterns
- hormonal signaling
- stress response chemistry
- brain-body signaling interfaces

## Core Claim
Psychological interpretation and physiological regulation are linked through neurochemical processes.

## Why It Matters
NcM acts as the bridge between internal interpretation and biological state.

## Typical Use
Use NcM in biological, psychological, and regulation-focused sections of ACL.

---

# The Perception Filter Model

## Definition
A model describing how humans interpret reality through sensory input, thought, emotion, and cognitive bias.

## Core Sequence
Reality  
→ System State  
→ Perception Filter  
→ Meaning  
→ Beliefs  
→ Behavior

## Core Claim
Humans do not respond to raw reality directly. They respond to interpreted reality.

## Why It Matters
This model explains how perception shapes meaning and behavior, especially under different states of regulation or threat.

## Typical Use
Use this model in:

- emotional regulation discussions
- neurodiversity materials
- growth and interpretation frameworks
- explanatory content for stress and survival responses

---

# The ACL Human Adaptive Cycle

## Definition
A cyclical model describing how humans move from regulation toward integration and outward care.

## Stages
Safety  
Capacity  
Clarity  
Authenticity  
Progress  
Meaning  
Beliefs  
Care  
Gratitude

## Summary Sequence
**Regulation → Alignment → Growth → Integration**

## Core Claim
Human growth is not linear willpower. It emerges through adaptive sequencing rooted in safety and regulation.

## Why It Matters
This model provides the developmental and growth-oriented center of ACL.

## Typical Use
Use this model in:

- growth pages
- coaching or educational materials
- public-facing explanations of regulation and development
- Hy-Kin integration materials

# GSS — Global Safety Standard

## Definition
A governance model defining when systems are structurally safe and valid.

## Core Function
GSS provides non-negotiable standards for determining whether a human system is:

- safe enough to operate
- structurally coherent
- repairable or invalid

## Why It Matters
GSS is the canonical safety model beneath all ACL work.

## Typical Use
Use GSS wherever structural safety, governance, standards, or social-system validity are being discussed.

---

# Emotional Physics (EP)

## Definition
A descriptive and corrective framework examining how emotional states behave in human systems under safe and unsafe conditions.

## Core Function
EP explains how emotional load, compression, misclassification, and recovery operate in biological and relational systems.

## Why It Matters
EP extends ACL into the lived emotional mechanics of human experience.

## Typical Use
Use EP in psychological, educational, relational, and nervous-system-related contexts.

---

# Societal Thermodynamics

## Definition
A systems-level theory proposing that safety functions as a thermodynamic substrate of civilization.

## Core Claim
When safety degrades, social entropy rises. As entropy rises, coherence, trust, and civilizational stability degrade.

## Why It Matters
This is ACL’s macro-scale framework for institutional and societal failure dynamics.

## Typical Use
Use this in:

- societal analysis
- safety governance
- failure dynamics discussions
- civilization-scale repair models

# Model Relationships

## How the models fit together

### GSS
Defines structural safety requirements.

### The Regulation Principle
Defines the core systems logic beneath health and stability.

### Perception Filter Model
Explains how interpreted reality is formed.

### NcM
Explains how interpretation affects chemistry and physiology.

### Biological Regulation
Describes the body-level coordination systems.

### Human Adaptive Cycle
Describes the growth sequence made possible by regulation.

### Emotional Physics
Describes emotional load, pressure, and recovery dynamics.

### Societal Thermodynamics
Extends the logic of regulation and entropy to civilizations and institutions.

---

# Use Rule

When explaining ACL to a new reader, the preferred order is:

1. GSS — structural safety
2. The Regulation Principle
3. Perception Filter Model
4. NcM — Neurochemical Modulation
5. Human Adaptive Cycle
6. Emotional Physics
7. Societal Thermodynamics

This sequence gives the clearest progression from safety to regulation to growth to large-scale systems.
