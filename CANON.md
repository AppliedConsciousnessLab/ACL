# Canonical Declaration — Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL)

This document defines the canonical status of the materials contained
within the Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL) repository.

Its purpose is to establish clarity, reduce conceptual drift, and
preserve long-term stability of the ACL research framework.

---

# Canonical Authority

The Applied Consciousness Lab (ACL) repository serves as the
**core research library** of the ACL ecosystem.

Materials published here define the foundational concepts used
across all related ACL repositories.

These concepts may be referenced, expanded, or tested in other
repositories, but the canonical definitions originate here.

---

# Foundational Sequence

ACL research is organized around a simple ordering principle:

Safety → Regulation → Agency → Choice

This sequence describes the conditions required for stable human systems.

1. Safety enables regulation  
2. Regulation enables agency  
3. Agency enables meaningful choice  

Systems that violate this order tend toward instability.

### Societal Thermodynamics

Explores how safety functions as the thermodynamic substrate of
civilizational stability.

Repository  
https://github.com/AppliedConsciousnessLab/SOCIETAL_THERMODYNAMICS

---

### Neurochemical Modulation (NcM)

Describes how changes in brain chemistry influence physiological
regulation and system behavior.

NcM acts as a bridge between psychological states and biological systems.

---

### The Regulation Principle

Proposes that health and stability emerge from coordinated
interaction between biological regulatory systems.

These include:

- nervous system regulation
- immune signaling
- endocrine feedback
- metabolic control
- epigenetic modulation

Breakdowns in regulatory coherence produce instability
at biological, psychological, and societal scales.

# Canonical Stability

ACL frameworks are intentionally conservative.

New ideas may be explored in experimental repositories,
but canonical material should change only when:

- evidence strongly supports revision
- conceptual clarity improves
- compatibility with existing frameworks is preserved

The goal is **long-term interpretability**, not rapid expansion.
