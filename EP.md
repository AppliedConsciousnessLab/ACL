# Emotional Physics (EP)

## Purpose

Emotional Physics (EP) is a descriptive framework for understanding how emotional states influence perception, behavior, and system stability.

EP treats emotion as an energetic and informational force that follows observable patterns under varying conditions of safety and threat.

EP is not a belief system.  
EP is not therapy.  
EP is not a medical or psychological diagnosis.

---

## Core Premise

Human systems respond to emotional pressure the way physical systems respond to force.

When safety is present, emotional energy supports:
- Curiosity
- Learning
- Cooperation
- Creativity
- Recovery

When safety is absent, emotional energy reorganizes toward survival.

This reorganization is lawful, not random.

---

## Relationship to Safety

Emotional Physics operates within the boundaries defined by the Global Safety Standards (GSS).

Safety determines the phase space in which emotional dynamics occur.

Without safety:
- Emotional signals amplify threat perception
- Memory and meaning distort
- Identity hardens or fragments
- Short-term survival overrides coherence

EP explains *how* degradation unfolds.  
GSS explains *why* it must be addressed first.

---

## Degradation Patterns

Under sustained unsafety, EP predicts:
- Escalation of fear-based signaling
- Loss of nuance and complexity
- Reactive decision-making
- Suppression of empathy
- Increased volatility or shutdown

These are adaptive responses to threat, not character flaws.

---

## Recovery and Coherence

When safety is restored:
- Emotional energy re-stabilizes
- Cognitive flexibility returns
- Truth tolerance increases
- Learning resumes
- Agency rebuilds

Recovery follows the same lawful patterns as degradation, in reverse.
